5 tips to stay HEALTHY this CHRISTMAS 🤶

The Christmas season is a time of joy, but it can also be a time of overindulgence. With all the parties, presents, treats and meals, it's easy to forget about looking after your health. To help you out, here are five simple steps you can take to ensure you stay healthy this Christmas:

1) Get Plenty of Rest – One key to maintaining good health this Christmas is getting plenty of rest. During this busy and often stressful time of year, it is easy to neglect our sleep. To get enough rest during the holidays, follow these tips:
👉 Schedule time for sleep: Try going to bed at a consistent time each night and wake up at the same time each morning.
👉 Avoid napping late in the day: this can interfere with your nighttime sleep routine.
👉 Minimize distractions: turn off electronics and any other distractions that can interfere with a peaceful night’s rest.
👉 Create a comfortable sleeping environment: Keep it cool, dark and quiet.

2) Eat a Healthy Diet – During the holidays, it can be tempting to indulge in unhealthy snacks and treats, but it’s important to remember to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits and vegetables. Eating a healthy diet is one of the most important ways to maintain your physical and mental wellbeing. Try to plan ahead.

3) Physical Exercise – If you want to stay healthy this Christmas, exercise should be at the top of your list. Some tips to get enough exercise this holiday include:
👉 Make a Plan: plan out how much time you’ll dedicate to exercise each week.
👉 Get Moving: Take walk or jog. Join a local gym or try an online exercise.
👉 Mix it Up: Variety is key when it comes to exercise. It helps to avoid boredom.
👉 Find a Workout Buddy: Family or group workout makes it more fun.

4) Limit Alcohol Consumption – We tend to consume lots of alcohol during the holiday season. To help ensure your health and well-being, try to limit your alcohol intake. Also, try to avoid high-calorie beverages such as creamy cocktails, instead, stick with wine or light beer. Stick to 1-2 drinks per day and avoid binge drinking.

5) Avoid Smoking – Smoking is one of the worst habits that we can fall into around the holidays. Smoking increases your risk of developing serious health conditions like cancer and heart disease. To ensure you stay healthy this Christmas, avoid smoking. Also, Avoid Secondhand Smoking. Avoid areas where people are smoking. Make your home a smoke-free zone.
